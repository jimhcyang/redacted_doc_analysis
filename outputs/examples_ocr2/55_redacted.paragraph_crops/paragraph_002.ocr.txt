Frank Marshall, Costa Rican revolutionary veteran, warned President Eschandi on 17 June that he has a substantial military force in the country ready to attack Nicaragua and that if Eschandi attempts to stop it, "there will be plenty of trouble in Costa Rica." The President immediately called in his cabinet and the leaders of various political parties. Fragmentary Costa Rican news-casts have subsequently indicated that, despite the threat, Eschandi may be sending elements of the Civil Guard to prevent an attack on Nicaragua by Marshall.
