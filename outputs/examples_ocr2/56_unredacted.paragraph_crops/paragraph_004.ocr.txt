Diaz Lanz, who was prominent among those officers seriously preoccupied with Communist penetration of the armed forces, is believed to have been particularly worried about the Communist influence in the recently established schools that have been giving six-week courses in political indoctrination to many Cuban military personnel. Diaz Lanz' half brother approached the US Embassy on 1 June for an opinion as to whether anti-Communist Cuban officers should go into exile or remain to resist Raul Castro's policies favoring the Communists.
