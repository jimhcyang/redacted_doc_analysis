

Syrian Communist leader Khalid Bakdash and Iraqi Kurdish dissident Mulla Mustafa Barzani have returned from the Soviet bloc. Bakdash, who like Barzani is of Kurdish origin, led his party in opposition to Egyptian-Syrian union and left Syria. For Prague and the USSR at the time of the UAR's formation last February. He reportedly returned to Damascus on 5 October. Barzani, who returned to Baghdad on 6 October, had been in the USSR since leading an unsuccessful Kurdish rebellion against Iraq and Iran in 1946. He stopped first at Cairo on 3 October, where he met with Nasir.
